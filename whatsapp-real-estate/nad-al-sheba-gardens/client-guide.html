Price trend

Up on last year, cooler than the spring peak

Dubai villa market trend · June 2025 – June 2026

The wider Dubai villa index cooled about 2.6% this quarter after a strong 2025. Nad Al Sheba Gardens itself is transacting at an average of AED 2,153 per sq ft — well above the market index and the highest in this group — with its premium villas in Phase 11 clearing AED 2,760–2,940 per sq ft.

Community

Premium, low-density, minutes from the city

Nad Al Sheba Gardens is a gated Meraas community in the Nad Al Sheba / Meydan district — designed as a low-density, green, family enclave, and one of the most centrally located villa communities in Dubai.

Its edge is location and brand: just 20 minutes to Dubai International and a short drive to Downtown, delivered by Meraas. The market is active across the board — 25 sales in a month, from AED 1.35M apartments in La Vue and Ethan up to a AED 17M plot and a AED 15.6M five-bedroom villa.

The decision

Sell now, or hold? An honest view

If you're considering selling

  • Your psf is the story. At an average AED 2,153/sq ft, this is premium territory. Large villas in Phase 11 are clearing AED 2,760–2,940/sq ft — we price to your exact phase and plot.
  • Central scarcity. There are very few low-density villa communities this close to the city, which underpins demand from end-users who want space without leaving central Dubai.
  • Both markets are live. Villas, apartments and plots are all transacting — a healthy sign of depth for sellers.

If you're leaning to hold

  • Central location scarcity and the Meraas name make this a strong long-term hold, especially as surrounding Meydan infrastructure matures.
  • The apartment lines (La Vue, Ethan, The Roof) give the community a broad, liquid rental base.
  • The smart move today is simply knowing your number, so you can act the moment it suits you.
