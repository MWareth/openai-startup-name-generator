# 🎙️ Multilingual Recruiting Spots — Studio Kit
**Hire bilingual agents · one short spot per language · English + their language**

Goal: attract people who speak **English + their own language** to join your
Dubai team. Each spot is ~20–30s, same English bookends, native invite in the
middle. Produce in HeyGen, caption in CapCut, post per-market.

---

## 🧩 The reusable structure (every spot)
1. **English hook (0–6s):** "Do you speak **[Language]** and English? Dubai is calling."
2. **Native invite (6–18s):** the message below, in that language.
3. **English CTA (18–25s):** "If that's you — DM me **'DUBAI'** and let's build your career here."

**Native invite — base message (what each translation says):**
> "Now is the best time to start your real estate career in Dubai. No
> experience? I'll train you from zero — so when the market rises again, you'll
> be ready to shine. Come join my team."

Keep the English hook + CTA **identical across all spots** for brand recognition.
Swap only the middle. (HeyGen note: one avatar can speak every language, or pick
a presenter per market for authenticity.)

## 🎬 How to produce (per spot, ~10 min in HeyGen)
1. **HeyGen** → New video → pick an avatar (one for all, or one per market).
2. Script box: type the **English hook**, paste the **native invite**, then the
   **English CTA** as one script. Set the avatar's language/voice to match (HeyGen
   handles multilingual lip-sync).
3. Background: a Dubai office/skyline image (or green-screen → CapCut).
4. Generate → download.
5. **CapCut:** add burned-in captions (native + English), your logo bug, upbeat
   music, and an end card: "DM 'DUBAI' to join." Export **9:16**.
6. Post each spot in its market (LinkedIn, Insta, local groups) — **Recruiter**
   playbook has where + the DM templates.

## ⚠️ Keep it honest (recruiting trust)
- "Train you from zero" — make sure you actually onboard/mentor juniors.
- No income or visa guarantees. If perks have conditions, state them.
- Use voices/avatars you have the right to use (HeyGen's licensed library is fine).
